## 1. Zoning Certificate

The Zoning Certificate is issued by the City Planning and Development Office citing the zoning classification of the land based on Zoning Ordinance.

- **Classification:** Simple
- **Type of transaction:** G2C — for government services whose client is the transacting public
- **Who may avail:** All

**Requirements → where to secure**

1. Letter of Request → To be provided by the client
2. TCT — must be Certified True Copy → Register of Deeds
3. Tax Declaration (Current) → City Assessor's Office
4. Tax Receipt (Current) → City Treasury Office

**Fee:** ₱1,000.00

- \*No fees will be collected for Students, doing research projects

**Processing time (total):** 27 minutes

**Client steps / agency actions:**

1. Submit Letter of Request together with the checklist of requirements → Receiving Officer checks; if complete, submits to City Planning and Development Office for approval (5 minutes)
2. City Planning and Development Office checks/evaluates the request together with documents and either approves/approves request (5 minutes) — _Engr. Leonida A. Lagrisola, City Planning and Development Officer_
3. Processing of the Certification — Zoning Officer does research/verification, prepares the Zoning Certificate and the Order of Payment (2 hours) — _Engr. Armando Aguilar, Project Development Officer III_
4. Signing of the Zoning Certificate and Order of Payment — City Planning and Development Officer reviews and signs (5 minutes) — _Engr. Leonida A. Lagrisola, City Planning and Development Officer_
5. Releasing of Order of Payment — taxpayer makes the necessary payment at the Treasury Office and submits a photocopy of the Official Receipt to the releasing officer (5 minutes) — _Maril Adocal, Clerk III / Receiving Officer_
6. Issuance of Zoning Certificate — releasing officer issues the Zoning Certificate (5 minutes) — _Ricardo Punzalan, Administrative Aide III / Releasing Officer_

## 2. Zoning Clearance for Business Permit

The Zoning Clearance for Business Permit is issued by the City Planning and Development Office for new development of business.

- **Classification:** Simple
- **Type of transaction:** G2C — for government services whose client is the transacting public
- **Who may avail:** All

**Requirements → where to secure**

1. Business Permit Application Form → Business Permit and Licensing Office
   - \*Additional requirements may be requested depending on the type of business.

**Fee:**

- **Old Business (Renewal):** Fixed amount of ₱500.00
- **New Business:**
  1. Minimum amount of ₱300.00 for area 20 sqm and below
  2. For area 20 to 100 sqm: computed @ Area (sqm) × ₱5.00 plus ₱500.00 (Processing Fee)
  3. For area 101 sqm and above: computed @ Area (sqm) × ₱5.00 plus ₱1,000.00 (Processing Fee)
  4. Special Permit for temporary business: fixed amount of ₱300.00
  - The assessed amount will be reflected at the back portion of the application form.

**Processing time (total):** 15 minutes _(verify)_

**Client steps / agency actions:**

1. Proceed to the City Planning and Development Office — receiving officer will check the requirement (5 minutes each transaction) — _Maril Adocal, Clerk II / Receiving Officer; Page Pagtakhan, Administrative Aide II / Receiving Officer_
2. Review of Documents — Zoning Officer reviews the documents; if it needs to secure a Locational Clearance or not; if it is needed to secure Locational Clearance, follow the steps on securing Locational Clearance (5 minutes) — _Engr. Armando Aguilar, Project Development Officer III_
3. Assessment of Fees (if found to be conforming) — receiving officer assesses the fees (5 minutes) — _Maril Adocal, Clerk II / Receiving Officer; Page Pagtakhan, Administrative Aide II / Receiving Officer_

## 3. Zoning / Locational Clearance

The Zoning / Locational Clearance is issued by the City Planning and Development Office to a project that is allowed under the provisions of Zoning Ordinance.

- **Classification:** Simple
- **Type of transaction:** G2C — for government services whose client is the transacting public
- **Who may avail:** All

**Requirements → where to secure**

1. One (1) Set of Plan (with signed and sealed: Architect/Civil Engr., Sanitary and Electrical Engr.) → To be provided by the client
2. Lot Plan with Vicinity Map (with signed & sealed) → To be provided by the client
3. TCT — must be Certified True Copy → Register of Deeds
4. Tax Declaration (Current) → City Assessor's Office
5. Tax Receipt (Current) → City Treasury Office
6. Barangay Clearance → Barangay
7. Neighbor's Consent → From Neighbors
8. Homeowner's Certification → Homeowner's Association

- \*Other requirements may be requested on a case-to-case basis.

**Fee:**

- **Commercial:** ₱2,000.00 (Filing fee) plus Area (Sq.m.) × ₱5.00 (Processing Fee)
- **Residential:** ₱2,000.00 (Filing fee) plus Area (Sq.m.) × ₱5.00 plus ₱3.00 _(verify — fee schedule partially legible)_

**Processing time (total):** 3 days

**Client steps / agency actions:**

1. Secure Application Form and list of Requirements from the City Planning and Development Office — _Eric Divingracia, Administrative Aide III / Receiving Officer; Maril Adocal, Clerk II / Receiving Officer_ (5 minutes, Day 1)
2. Submit the completely filled out Notarized Application Form together with complete set of Requirements for initial checking of the receiving officer — receiving officer will check the requirements; if complete, it will be scheduled for ocular inspection (5 minutes, Day 1) — _Eric Divingracia, Administrative Aide III / Receiving Officer; Maril Adocal, Clerk II / Receiving Officer_
3. Ocular Inspection of the proposed projects — Zoning Inspectors will inspect the proposed projects (Half Day A.M., Day 2) — _Danje Sabiaga, Project Development Officer II; Vladimere Ilustre, Administrative Aide II_
4. Writing of Inspection Report — Zoning Inspectors will write an inspection report of the proposed projects (5 minutes, Day 2) — _Danje Sabiaga, Project Development Officer II; Vladimere Ilustre, Administrative Aide II_
5. Evaluation / Checking by Zoning Officers — Zoning Officer evaluates and checks the proposed projects (20 minutes, Day 2) — Commercial Projects: _Engr. Armando Aguilar, Project Development Officer III_; Residential Projects: _Arch. Bernard Alvarez, Project Development Officer II_
6. Processing and Checking of Zoning / Locational Clearance — Zoning Officers does check and verification, prepares the Zoning / Locational Clearance and the Order of Payment (15 minutes, Day 2) — Commercial Projects: _Vladimere Ilustre, Administrative Aide II_; Residential Projects: _Ricardo Punzalan, Administrative Aide III_
7. Recommending Approval — Zoning Officers recommend for the approval of the Zoning / Locational Clearance of the proposed projects (5 minutes, Day 2) — Commercial Projects: _Engr. Armando Aguilar, Project Development Officer III_; Residential Projects: _Arch. Bernard Alvarez, Project Development Officer II_
8. Final Checking and Approval / Signing of Order of Payment — City Planning and Development Officer checks, approves and signs the Order of Payment (10 minutes, Day 2) — _Engr. Leonida A. Lagrisola, City Planning and Development Officer_
9. Releasing of Order of Payment — taxpayer makes the necessary payment at the Treasurer's Office and submits a photocopy of the Official Receipt to the releasing officer (5 minutes, Day 3) — _Ricardo Punzalan, Administrative Aide III / Releasing Officer_
10. Signing of Zoning / Locational Clearance — City Planning and Development Officer signs the Zoning / Locational Clearance (5 minutes of each clearance, Day 3) — _Engr. Leonida A. Lagrisola, City Planning and Development Officer_
11. End: Releasing of Approved Zoning / Locational Clearance — releasing officer issues the Zoning / Locational Clearance (5 minutes of each clearance, Day 3) — _Ricardo Punzalan, Administrative Aide III / Releasing Officer_

## Feedback and Complaints Mechanism

- **How to send feedback:** Answer client feedback form and drop it at the designated drop box in front of the City Public Relations & Information Office. Contact Info: **8871-4343 / 8871-4345**, www.laspinascity.gov.ph
- **How feedbacks are processed:** The Public Relation Officer compiles and records all feedback submissions. Feedback requiring answers are forwarded to the relevant offices and they are required to answer within three (3) days of the receipt of the feedback. The answer of the office is relayed to the citizen. For inquiries and follow ups, client may contact the following telephone number: **8871-4343 / 8871-4345**.
- **How to file a complaint:** Answer the clients Complaint Form and drop it at the designated drop box in front of the City Public Relations & Information Office. Complaint can also be filed via telephone. Make sure to provide the following information: Name of person being complained, Incident, Evidence. For inquiries and follow ups, client may contact the following telephone number: **8871-4343 / 8871-4345**. The Complaints Officer opens the complaints drop box on a daily basis and evaluates each complaints. Upon evaluation, the Complaints Officer shall start the investigation and forward the complaint to the relevant office for their explanation. The Complaints Officer will create a report after the investigation and shall submit it to the Head of particular office for appropriate action. The Complaints Officer will give the feedback to the client. For inquiries and follow ups, client may contact the following telephone number: **8871-4343 / 8871-4345**.
- **Contact (ARTA, PCC, CCB):** ARTA: complaints@arta.gov.ph; PCC: 8478-5093; CCB: 0908-881-6565 (SMS)
